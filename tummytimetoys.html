The Only Tummy Time Toys That Got My Daughter to Lift Her Head

All Things Baby  ·  By Steph

My daughter hated tummy time. Really hated it. We knew she needed it for development — neck strength, eventually rolling and crawling — so we kept trying, and kept watching her face-plant and cry within thirty seconds. We did the high-contrast cards, the rattles, the little mirror propped up in front of her face. Everyone recommends those. They did nothing for us.

These three toys are the only things that actually worked.

The Crawling Crab

Noisy and clacky and distracting in all the right ways. I started flipping it on its back so she could watch the legs moving in the air — she'd just lock onto it and stare. Something about the movement got her every time in a way that the usual tummy time props never did.

The Dancing Talking Cactus

The voice-repeat feature is what makes this one. I'd call out from across the room and it would echo back — she'd freeze with her head fully lifted, completely puzzled, trying to figure out where the sound was coming from. Worked like a charm every single time.

Fisher-Price Kick & Play Piano Gym

Honestly, I thought this looked overpriced and a little unnecessary when I first saw it. I was completely wrong. I don't know what Fisher-Price put into this thing, but every baby I know is obsessed with it. The songs, the colors dancing across the piano keys — we got ten straight minutes of tummy time the first time we used it. It's still her favorite toy months later. If you're on the fence, get it.

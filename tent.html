Baby Blackout Tents: A Complete Game-Changer

The product that actually gives your baby their own room — anywhere

All Things Baby  ·  By Steph

I'll be honest — when someone first told me to look into baby blackout sleep tents, I almost didn't. The price felt insane for what looked like, I don't know, a glorified canopy. Then we traveled with our daughter for the first time and I got it immediately.

These things are a lifesaver. Whether you're at a hotel, at the in-laws', or just have people over and need the house to still function like a house — your baby has her own room wherever she goes. We were also genuinely shocked by how much noise they block, which isn't something people talk about enough. Combined with the darkness, it's the closest thing to actually replicating her nursery that we've found.

Here's how the three main options compare.

1

SlumberPod

#1 Pick
The one that keeps coming up — because it actually does what it says.
  • Four ventilation panels, two fan pockets, a monitor pocket — the details are all there
  • Independently tested for CO2 safety, which I didn't know to look for until I went down the rabbit hole on these
  • The newer version includes a fan, which matters
  • Fits over most travel cribs and pack n plays and packs down small enough that it's not a whole thing to travel with
  • Sleep consultants recommend it constantly — and once you use it you understand why
  • One thing I didn't expect: it seemed to help with our four month sleep regression. I don't know if it was the darkness or just that she felt so cozy and enclosed in her own little space, but something about it clicked for her. Could be coincidence, but I'm not willing to test that theory.

2

Hiccapop Daydreamer

#2 Pick
A close second and a real contender.
  • Six air vents and color-coded clips that make setup pretty intuitive
  • Comes with a lifetime guarantee that covers accidental damage — which honestly surprised me, I didn't expect that
  • The carry bag fits in a suitcase, which is a nice detail
  • Blocks about 90% of light versus 95%+ on the SlumberPod — you do notice the difference
  • Fan isn't included, which feels like an oversight at this price
Shop on Amazon →
3

YABORAS

#3 Pick
If budget is the deciding factor, this is the one.
  • The one-click setup is genuinely the easiest of the three — takes about two minutes once you figure out the folding
  • Ventilation is decent and the materials feel fine
  • Not fully blackout — the fabric leans more breathable than truly dark, so you get dim rather than pitch black
  • Doesn't have the same safety certifications or track record as the other two

If you're going to get one, get the SlumberPod. We use ours every time we travel and honestly more at home than I expected.

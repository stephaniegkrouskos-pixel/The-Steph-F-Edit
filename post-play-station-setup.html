4 Months Old & Ready to Set Up Your Play Station?

Here's what's working for me

All Things Baby  ·  By Steph

It's time to set up a play mat and playpen in your home, but you aren't sure what products to choose through all the noise on Amazon and TikTok — I feel this. Before we get into specifics, know that there are two types of moms out there and I respect them both: the beige/interior design mom and the mom who happily lets the whole house be taken over by baby gear. I'm somewhere in between.

I hated the idea of a playpen eating my living room, but it also wasn't practical to not have her setup near my main workspace — the kitchen. So I set out to find pieces that were functional, sizeable enough to actually be useful, and didn't make me feel like my home had completely surrendered to babyhood. Here's what I landed on.

What to prioritize

Before I get into the specific products, these were my two non-negotiables going into the search:

  • ✦ Enough space for baby to move around freely and not feel fully caged — this matters more than you'd think as they start becoming more active
  • ✦ A pen that feels fun and has built-in engagement for baby, but also folds up nicely so it isn't a permanent eyesore

With those in mind, here's what I found and actually use every day.

The Playpen: Wooden Extra-Large Baby Playpen

The Pen
🌿 Big enough to actually matter — and it looks good doing it.
  • Genuinely large footprint — baby has real room to roll, kick, and eventually move around without feeling contained
  • Toys are built into the walls (an abacus, sliding beads, shapes) — she is obsessed and it keeps her engaged independently
  • The light natural wood aesthetic actually looks nice in a living space — doesn't scream "baby took over"
  • Folds up when not in use, which was a must for me
  • Assembly isn't the easiest — totally doable, but block out about 40 minutes and don't rush it

The Mat: House of Noa Little Nomad Play Mat

The Mat
✨ I went with the 8×6 Dover Chestnut — and I'd pick it again without hesitation.
  • The 8×6 size is the sweet spot — large enough that baby has real estate inside the pen, and you can always size down in a new space
  • So many colors and patterns to choose from — this is the piece that helps strike the balance between "I have taste" and "my baby actually uses this"
  • Padded and easy to wipe clean, which is everything at this stage
  • The Dover Chestnut colorway is subtle and warm — blends beautifully with natural wood tones and doesn't clash with the rest of your space

If you're on the fence about sizing for the mat, go bigger. You can always adapt it to a smaller space later, and the extra room makes such a difference for how much your baby actually enjoys the setup. The last thing you want is to feel like you need to upgrade in two months.

Overall, I'm really happy with how this little corner of our home came together — functional, not an eyesore, and she genuinely loves being in there. That's the win.

Swaddles, Sacks & Suits

The ultimate guide to what to buy — and when to transition

As a first-time mom, this was honestly one of the most confusing things to figure out. There are so many brands and nobody just tells you what actually works. I ended up buying one from every brand and wasting so much money before landing on what we actually loved. So consider this the guide I wish I'd had — straight from me and my entire mom circle — on exactly what to buy and when to make the switch.

0 – 6/8 weeks

Swaddle Time

Top Pick

Kyte Baby Bamboo Swaddle

Seemed pricey at first, but every baby in our group was most comfortable in this one — and it was the only swaddle none of them could escape from. The bamboo fabric is incredibly soft and breathable, which matters a lot in those early weeks.

Budget Alternative

Halo Swaddle

A solid runner-up if you want to save a little money. We never quite got the same quality night sleep as with the Kyte, but it's a reasonable option if you're watching the budget.

6/8 weeks ish

The Magic Merlin Suit

Top Pick

Baby Merlin's Magic Sleepsuit

The name says it all. At this stage, baby still has the startle reflex that wakes them up, but they're starting to want a little more freedom than a swaddle allows. The Merlin suit hits that sweet spot perfectly — it cushions those jerky movements without fully restricting their arms. Bonus: your baby will look like the cutest little Michelin Man you've ever seen.

When baby starts rolling

Sleep Sack Season

At this point, the specific brand matters less — what we cared most about was clean ingredients. We stuck to natural materials like organic cotton and wool to keep things safe and breathable against baby's skin.

Short Sleeve Pick

Burt's Bees Baby Organic Cotton Sleep Sack

Our go-to for short-sleeve wear. Made from 100% GOTS-certified organic cotton — no harsh dyes or synthetic finishes — and one of the most affordable options out there. The fit is generous enough that baby has plenty of room to kick and move.

Shop on Amazon →
Long Sleeve Pick

Kaiya Baby Long Sleeve Sleep Sack

Our favorite for long-sleeve wear, mainly for the weight and feel of the fabric. Kaiya uses a bamboo-cotton blend that's naturally temperature-regulating, hypoallergenic, and incredibly soft — all things that matter when something is against your baby's skin all night. It's also free of harmful chemicals and safe for sensitive skin.

Pro tip: keep a long-sleeve sack in rotation even if baby wears short sleeves during the day. If they like having their arms covered for naps, this is your answer.
